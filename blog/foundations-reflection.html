Core Skills Learning and Foundations

Reflection on the Core Skills of Foundations

What are three new things you have learnt about yourself and your ego due to the core learning?

  • I am very self critical
  • My determination will take me far
  • It is okay to be vulnerable and grow from it

What are the role of values, empathy, and self-awareness in learning and programming?

Values are important in learning, programming, and every aspect of life. They are what keeps us accountable for being the person we are - the stronger your values, the stronger you push to uphold those

Empathy is so important and this can apply to empathy for others who are on the learning journey as well, or empathy for yourself. To show yourself kindness is a strength, and for me I need a constant reminder to do this. I’m sure it will get easier with practice!

Self-awareness is another huge skill - this plays a role in identifying your own strengths and weaknesses - if you can identify those you can then adapt and utilise those strengths, and work on the weaknesses.. For example I have found that I can work really well in the evening - so I do some study between 9 and midnight and it’s some of my more focused work. I didn’t get the opportunity for that working at a 9-5!

What has surprised you the most about the core learning?

How vulnerable and openly I can write, knowing I’m going to put it on the internet and near strangers will read it. I’ve given it my all in putting all of my thoughts/feelings/self into what I write. I want to be true to myself, and i’m figuring that out as I go

What were the most challenging aspects of the core learning?

Feeling all the feelings - some of it was really difficult emotionally. One part that stood out to me as the hardest was the identity and values blog. I had to really analyse who I am, what makes me who I am, as well as who do I want to be? That will still be a process for now, but that was a really big step

Why do you think we, a programming school, are spending so much time focusing on core learning in a web development Bootcamp course?

I see it as an investment in the whole person of a student at Dev Academy. A programmer is not just a programmer, but also a thinker, a learner, a teacher, a leader. A human being. Students moving on to a programming career will most likely be working in a team - and a team needs well rounded individuals to operate at its best. This increases employability, as well as maintaining a reputation for training people as such. Another benefit of learning core is actually preparing for Bootcamp - getting in touch with oneself will only benefit the student in the next stage as well as improve the whole experience for the individual
