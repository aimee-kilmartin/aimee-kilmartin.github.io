What is my wellbeing plan?

My Te Whare Tapa Whā Wellbeing Plan

These are my goals and plan for my wellbeing using the Te Whare Tapa Whā model. This will no doubt be a work in progress, and I hope to strengthen all of these areas for my wellbeing.

Taha Tinana (Physical Wellbeing)

Walk three times a week with my dog - I will modify this to one long walk per weekend during bootcamp as I don’t like walking her in the dark. This also gives a boost to my Taha Hinengaro as I feel at peace when I walk with her, and I connect to nature as well.

I’m keen to get into the yoga during bootcamp, and will try some out beforehand at home too!

I try to cook and eat healthy meals to provide my body with adequate nutrition - having a balanced diet but not restrictive either. I want to enjoy my food, not calorie count it!

Taha Whānau (Family Wellbeing)

One thing that is really important to me is spending dinner time with my family - being my tight-knit home unit with my partner, my cousin and a good friend. We aren’t all home every night of the week, but most evenings we spend time together cooking, eating and catching up on our days.

Taha Hinengaro (Emotional and Mental Wellbeing)

This is probably my most vulnerable wall. I lean on my home-family for support during the tough times, but have few close relationships outside of these people. This journey with Dev Academy is a chance for me to really work on myself, starting with resigning a stressful job and making a positive change in my life to do something meaningful for me.

Some things I do to help calm myself or feel accomplished is to create something. I have learned this about myself in the recent-ish past, where I feel good having made something. It can be baking, painting a cabinet, just about anything that uses some creativity. I am seeing that coding will also fall into this!

Taha Wairua (Spiritual Wellbeing)

Get to the beach as much as I can. Unfortunately this is a hard one especially in winter - but sitting or walking at the beach and watching the sunset, or simply enjoying the sounds of the waves has a grounding affect on me. I will enjoy the connection to nature at the beach too.

I am interested in connecting with my Iwi as well - something I have been looking into this year.

Whenua (Interconnections to the land and environment)

I tend to notice and appreciate nature around me often - I am lucky enough to enjoy both the sunrise and sunset from my living room/current desk! I will see at least one of these every day, even during bootcamp I will catch the sunrise.

I also have some indoor plants I tend to, and a small vege garden that provides me with food so long as I put the love into it.
