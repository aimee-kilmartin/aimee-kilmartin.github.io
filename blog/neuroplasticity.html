Neuroplasticity

Neuroplasticity Principles and Benefits

Understanding neuroplasticity benefits people by removing the ‘can’t’ from people’s thinking. This links in with the growth mindset below as well, because with both of these together the limitations put on us are removed.

We are each born with a certain amount of neurons in our brain - they can become damaged and we cannot repair or regenerate our neurons. However, what we can generate is our neural pathways - this is what connects the neurons and these pathways are activated from using our memory and actions. Neuroplasticity is the malleability of the brain via repetition of an action, phrase or thought that strengthens this neural pathway.

Understanding that this is within our control benefits everyone whenever they want to learn something new - from a new language or paper at university, through to how to boil an egg or pronounce a name. It doesn’t matter how big or small the thing is! These things become easier over time as we strengthen the relevant pathways in our brain.

Of the principles found in my research (commonly 10 principles were referred to) I found these the most relevant to my learning:

  • Use it and improve it: Neural pathways grow stronger the more they are used
  • Specificity: Your brain forms a specific circuitry in response to specific activities
  • Time matters: It takes varying amounts of time for the brain to change depending on how complex or foreign the new behavior or skill is to you

When we learn, we create and strengthen these pathways, allowing us to use our knowledge at a later stage. Our pathways can be strengthened both by ‘doing’ the thing we are wanting to learn, and by thinking about doing it - ‘mental practice’.

I will apply this to my learning with Dev Academy by practicing, both physically and mentally. Thinking about what I am doing, why and how both during an exercise and after. Knowing that putting things into practice will strengthen my learning, will help motivate me to be in control of this experience, that it is in my hands. I will make the most of the time I have, use repetition and specificity to strengthen my learning.

What are some of the ways to increase neuroplasticity?

Increasing neuroplasticity can be done through both physical and mental practice - and even via mental practice for a physical task!

Some common ways to improve neuroplasticity are:

  • Learning a new language: HTML, CSS, and Javascript count right?!
  • Exercise: Going for a walk or doing strength exercises boosts brain activity, and has a bunch of other health benefits too.
  • Meditation: Practicing mindfulness and meditation can also increase the connections between left and right brain.
  • Video Games: These practice problem solving, resilience, decision-making among other things.

Recently my flatmate pulled out a video game (released 20 years ago) on an old PS2. I used to play the game as a kid and I was amazed at how much I remembered about the navigation and placement of locations having not played it in many, many, many years. The neural pathway suddenly came alive and knew what to do

Growth Mindset

What is a Growth Mindset and why is it important?

Growth mindset is important as it allows us out of the ‘box’ of one's potential achievement levels. Grouping people into ‘smart’ ‘average’ and ‘dumb’ doesn't help ANYONE in those groups - it fosters comparison amongst peers, putting pressure on those ahead to perform to the standard, and giving others a perceived limit on their abilities.

Growth mindset doesn’t separate people by their current achievement standard, but allows everyone to put the fate of their learning into their own hands, armed with the knowledge that hard work and dedication will improve their success, skill, and/or intelligence.

The principle of ‘not yet’ is also powerful as it leaves the opening for future development, rather than shutting it down with a ‘fail’. This gives the opportunity to keep trying, keep developing, and keep going

What does Growth Mindset mean for me and my journey?

This concept of growth mindset is both new and foreign for me. I’d never heard the term before - but always believed in the concept that humans can always learn new things - it just may not be easy!

What has changed for me the most in researching this is the belief in myself. If everyone else can do it, so can I! I tend to be hard on myself, and particularly the ‘not yet’ resonated as something I want to focus on.

In each challenge, every time I don’t understand or get stuck I will remind myself that though I haven't got it yet, I will with enough practice. I will also add to my morning mindfulness, a little reminder to myself that I am learning, literally growing my brain, and that I can do this! There is no ‘not good enough’ just ‘not yet’.
